More About

Up until the pandemic shut down public performances, I was a touring singer-songwriter with a long-time coding hobby. Now those roles have been reversed.

I have been teaching myself computer programming languages as a hobby for the last 40 years, starting with machine language, MS/DOS scripts, CBASIC and dBase in the 80s. When I morphed into a touring singer-songwriter after 2000, I built an Access/VBA customer relations and tour management database, upgrading and maintaining it for 12 years. It relied entirely on VBA code and SQL. It supported every aspect of our business and often involved writing complex SQL queries from scratch.

Hair-raising as it often was, I came to enjoy the challenge of ferreting out documentation, troubleshooting and debugging, at least when it was over. Wrong answers pursued lead to right answers. Bugs tracked down make for better coding. I also developed a real love for working with data, which led me into Python and R.
